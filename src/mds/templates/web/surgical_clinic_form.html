Please use the following definition of surgical site infection (SSI):

Infection occurs within 30 days after any operative procedure AND the patient has at least one of the following:

  • a. Purulent drainage from the incision.
  • b. Organisms isolated from a culture of fluid or tissue from the incision.
  • c. Incision that spontaneously dehisces or is deliberately opened by a surgeon.
  • d. Diagnosis of an SSI by the surgeon.

DO NOT report SSI if:

  • Stitch abscess: minimal inflammation and discharge confined to the points of suture penetration.
  • Cellulits: erythema without swelling, fluctuance or drainage from the incision.
    • Antibiotics
    • Open the incision and pack at the bedside
    • Drainage in the operating room - local anesthesia
    • Drainage in the operating room -general or spinal anesthesia
    • Readmission

Stop here if this is a routine post-op visit. Continue if the patient was referred for symptoms.
